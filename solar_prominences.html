solar prominences

Photoshop · Clip Studio Paint · 2026

In April 2026, researchers at the Max Planck Institute for Solar System Research published a landmark paper in Nature Astronomy titled "A Giant Leap for Virtual Solar Prominences"--the first simulation to model the full dynamics of solar prominence formation from sub-photosphere to corona. Solar prominences are enormous structures of cool, dense plasma suspended in the Sun's million-degree outer atmosphere by magnetic fields, persisting for days to weeks to months before erupting and sending charged particles toward Earth. This editorial illustration was made in response to that paper: an attempt to make the physics tangible and visible, while also conveying the beauty and strangeness of these otherworldly phenomena.

Final Illustrations

Three versions of the final illustration explore slightly different approaches to the same subject, both in narrative and in pure design. The core visual challenge was representing something that is simultaneously enormous in scale and delicate in structure--prominences span thousands of kilometers yet look like flickering flames. The versions experiment with different combinations of elements, from yarn draped over a pin to yarn creating an opening into space to try and find the right balance of context cues for unfamiliar viewers.
