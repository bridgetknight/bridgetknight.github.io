Final illustration — Photoshop, Clip Studio Paint

narrative environment concept

Photoshop · Clip Studio Paint · 2026

This project explores an abandoned sci-fi cityscape as a narrative space — a world that tells its story through light, atmosphere, and the absence of people. The environment was developed from concept to final illustration, using a 3D blockout in Blender to solve perspective and scale before committing to the digital painting. The goal was to make a place that feels lived-in and left behind: overgrown, quiet, and charged with the weight of something that happened here.

Final Illustration

The lighting was a supplementary narrative tool--film noir lighting in the foreground, and mysterious sci-fi lighting in the background to raise more questions. The narrative is primarily driven by the left-behind objects scattered throughout the image, creating an unclear timeline of events. The figure is absent by design--I aimed for the space to be more unsettling when there's no one to anchor it in reality.

3D Blockout

The 3D blockout in Clip Studio Paint was used purely as a perspective and scale tool--not a final render. Building the basic geometry first allowed me to lock in the camera angle and establish accurate spatial relationships before spending time on the painting.
