mass audubon — save the salt marsh

Photoshop · Illustrator · 2026

This conservation poster was made in support of Mass Audubon's Great Marsh restoration efforts, a multi-year initiative to restore tidal flow and habitat in the Great Marsh of northeastern Massachusetts. For 3 months, I worked as a field technician supporting these efforts, and learned that the Great Marsh is the largest contiguous salt marsh in New England, a critical habitat for migratory birds, marine nurseries, and coastal resilience. The poster was designed to communicate that urgency visually, to an audience that might not otherwise encounter conservation messaging.

Final Poster

The design draws on the visual language of vintage conservation and WPA posters--bold color fields, semi-flat illustration, strong typography--to evoke a sense of the marsh as a place worth protecting (however, I believe if you visit the Great Marsh you will understand why it's so worth protecting). The palette is pulled directly from the Great Marsh environment: tidal greens, marsh grasses, the blue-grey of the Atlantic sky in autumn, against the backdrop of an ocean-front sunset.
